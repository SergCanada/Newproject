Get yourself a new home

The first option you may want to consider is getting yourself a better place. If that sounds like you're straying from the goal of getting an investment property, you're not at all. Instead, you'll be using a convenient way to bypass the 20% down payment requirement altogether, since you'll only need to put 5% down for your new home. That's because even with the mortgage rule changes this year in March, Canadians can purchase a personal residence with just 5% down over a 30-year term. So once you find a nice, new place, just simply hang on to your last one and rent it out, says Michael Marini, a mortgage broker with Dominion Lending Centres. "It's an easy way for a first-time buyer to get into the rental market with as little as 5% down." But this cannot be done deliberately, meaning you cannot apply for a mortgage for a principal residence and then immediately rent it out. That would be mortgage fraud, he says. "It's absolutely illegal for someone to buy a property and indicate that they're going to own/occupy it and then rent it out." If the bank knew that was the buyer's intention, it wouldn't finance the mortgage without a 20% down payment.
