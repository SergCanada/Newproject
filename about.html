What is staging for?

If you want to sell a home quickly and for the best possible price, staging is one of the easiest ways to make it happen.

Staging a home makes it easier for buyers to recognize when a house meets their expectations. Buyers are encouraged (and rightfully so) to focus more on the “bones” of a house than its interior aesthetics. That can be tough to do though when clutter, outdated furniture, and loads of personal items attached to the current owners are on display in the listing pictures and during showings. As a seller, you have a lot to gain from making it easier on the buyer to see the true potential of a space—and to do it, you’re going to need to stage.

According to 83% of buyers’ agents, staging a home makes it easier for a buyer to visualize the property as a future home.

This is good news for sellers, who may have limited resources to put toward a full staging.

Staging a home ensures that buyers see it in its very best light, and can help illustrate what a property can offer without requiring a complete makeover.
